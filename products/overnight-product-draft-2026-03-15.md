## Part 1: 5 simple lead-handling rules

### Rule 1: Reply fast
A decent reply now is better than a perfect reply later.

### Rule 2: Ask for booking details early
Do not keep chatting without collecting the basic info you need.

### Rule 3: Keep messages short
Long blocks of text feel heavy. Short messages get more replies.

### Rule 4: Follow up calmly
Many people do not say no. They just get busy.

### Rule 5: Always end with one clear next step
Do not end with vague messages. Ask for one action.

Examples of clear next steps:
- “Please send your location.”
- “Which time do you prefer, 2pm or 5pm?”
- “Would you like me to lock in your slot?”

## Part 2: Copy-paste WhatsApp scripts

### A. First reply to a new inquiry

**Script 1: Fast general reply**

Hi, thanks for messaging [Business Name].

Yes, we can help with [service].
To give you an accurate quote, please send:
1. your area/location
2. what service you need
3. photos or short video if possible
4. preferred date/time

Once I have that, I’ll reply with the next step.

**Script 2: Fast reply when you are busy**

Hi, thanks for your message. I’m currently on a job, but I’ll get back to you as soon as I can.

To speed things up, please send:
- location
- service needed
- photos/video
- preferred timing

That way I can reply properly once free.

**Script 3: Availability-first reply**

Hi, yes, we’re available for [service].
Please send your location and preferred time, and I’ll check the best slot for you.

---

### B. Asking the right questions before quoting

**Script 4: Basic info request**

Thanks. Before I quote, I need a few quick details:
- exact location
- type of job
- size/quantity
- access details if any
- photos if available

Once I have that, I can advise properly.

**Script 5: For customers who only ask “How much?”**

Price depends on the job details, so I don’t want to guess and misquote you.

Send me:
- location
- what needs to be done
- photos if possible

Then I can give you a more accurate estimate.

---

### C. Sending the quote

**Script 6: Simple quote message**

Thanks for the details.

Estimated price for this job: [RM amount]
Available slot: [day/time]
What’s included: [short list]

If you’d like to proceed, I can reserve the slot for you.

**Script 7: Quote with choice**

Thanks, here are your options:

Option 1: [basic package] — [price]
Option 2: [better package] — [price]

If you want, I can recommend the best option based on your situation.

**Script 8: Quote with urgency but no pressure**

Estimated price is [RM amount].
At the moment, I still have [day/time] available.

If you want that slot, I can hold it for you once you confirm.

---

### D. Follow-up after sending quote

**Script 9: Same-day follow-up**

Hi, just checking if you saw my quote.
If you want, I can also help you choose the best option based on your budget.

**Script 10: Next-day follow-up**

Hi, just following up on your [service] inquiry.
Would you like to go ahead, or would you like me to answer any questions first?

**Script 11: Friendly nudge**

Hi, no pressure at all. Just checking whether you’d still like help with this.
If yes, send me your preferred day/time and I’ll advise the next step.

---

### E. Handling common objections

**Script 12: “Too expensive”**

Understood. If it helps, I can suggest the most cost-effective option based on what you need.
Do you want the lowest-price option or the best-value option?

**Script 13: “I need to ask my spouse / boss”**

No problem.
If helpful, I can summarize the price, scope, and timing in one short message that you can forward.

**Script 14: “I’ll let you know”**

Sure, no problem.
Would you like me to check back with you tomorrow, or would you prefer to message me when ready?

---

### F. Closing the booking

**Script 15: Soft close**

If you’re happy to proceed, send me:
- full name
- full address
- preferred date/time
- any special notes

Then I’ll confirm your booking details.

**Script 16: Confirm slot**

I can do [day/time].
If you want me to reserve it, please confirm now and send your address.

**Script 17: Booking confirmation**

Booking confirmed for:
- service: [service]
- date: [date]
- time: [time]
- location: [address]
- agreed price: [price]

If anything changes, please let me know early.

---

### G. Reactivating old leads

**Script 18: Old lead reactivation**

Hi, you contacted us earlier about [service].
Just checking whether you still need help.
If yes, send me your latest details and I’ll advise from there.

**Script 19: Promotion-style reactivation without fake urgency**

Hi, just reaching out in case you still need [service].
We’re still available this week, so if you want an updated quote, send me the current details.
